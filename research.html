Patchiness

To reproduce variability inside the hypoxic region, models need to resolve sub-mesoscale (100 m to 10 km) and mesoscale (10 to 100 km) dynamic processes. Hourly simulations of a simple oxygen parametrization incorporated in a high resolution hydrodynamic model, show the formation and destruction of eddy-like features and fronts in the bottom oxygen structure. Due to the difficulty of sampling, these features have not been fully observed but hinted by high-resolution Acrobat measurements (DiMarco and Zimmerle, 2017).

Hypoxic extent

Models allow a more dynamic overview of the development of the hypoxic region in the Texas-Louisiana Shelf. While annual cruise observations capture a static vision of what is expected to be the largest seasonal extent every year (late July or early August), models allow to see the development of the region. Although model simulations are not completely accurate, they still give an estimation driven by realistic hydrodynamic settings. 20 years of model output capture a wide range of interannual variability.

Oxygen budget

A budget analysis reveals different rates of contribution of the horizontal and vertical advective fluxes, and the vertical diffusive flux in the movement and formation of bottom water hypoxia. Relative contributions of these fluxes to the balance change at different spatial scales. While horizontal advective fluxes are responsible for the high-frequency variability seen in the oscillation of oxygen-depleted water masses, vertical diffusive fluxes, and bottom respiration dominate over longer spatial and temporal scales. Understanding the mechanisms of the different scales of variability simulated in the model can aid in improving observation strategies to evaluate the seasonal extent of hypoxia on the Texas-Louisiana shelf more accurately, as well as evaluate the ecological implications on a region subject to different oscillatory modes. .

Shelf properties

Property histograms help visualize the change of a water characteristics distibution on the shelf over time. The distribution of some properties (like vertical salinity gradient and surface vorticity) can explain some of the variability in the bottom water oxygen concentration. Vorticity values distanced from zero indicate the precense of instabilities in the shelf, wich are originated from the river plume fronts .
